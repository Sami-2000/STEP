My Projects:

In 2017, I programmed autonomous drones at MIT's Beaverworks Summer Institute. I worked in a small team to plan, program, and race an autonomous air vehicle. While there, I not only learned principles of computer vision, image recognition, and navigation, but also how to break down a large problem and communicate effectively on a team.

Starting in winter 2020, I used machine learning to analyze fly image data at the Clark Lab of Yale. The Clark Lab performs experiments on fruit flies in order to learn more about their precepts and map out their brain functionality. I analyzed images from an experiment which explored depth perception by examining how flies cross gaps of various width to identify the position and the behavior of the fly.

From winter 2019 through summer 2019, I worked in Yale's Ultracold Quantum Matter Lab. While there, I updated the control software for atomic physics experiments in order to increase compatibility with new hardware and software packages. I also wrote molecular dynamics simulations to better inform the trapping of Bose Einstein Condensates.
